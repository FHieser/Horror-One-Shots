# Mechanics

### 🌩️ The Eternal Storm & the Mountain Facility
The facility sits on a mountain peak, surrounded by an endless, unnatural snowstorm. Two energy towers flank the building, harvesting constant lightning strikes to power everything inside. The storm is the facility's heartbeat — it never weakens, never stops.

**When players venture outside:**
• The area has no other light source. Everything is visible only in lightning flashes — strobing, disorienting, never steady.
• The towers loom on both sides, briefly illuminated in full with each strike. Use them as landmarks and as dread.
• Play thunderstorm sounds from the moment players step outside. Keep them running until the players return indoors.

### 🧬 Cloning & Identity Horror

When a player dies, take them aside privately so the other players don't know what is happening.

**Step by step:**
• The player loses all equipment — it stays where they died.
• Note down their current Stress total.
• The player creates a completely new character: new class, new skills. Aim to be as random as possible. Embrace the change.
• The player keeps their colour codename. That is the only constant.
• When ready, the player wakes up in the Entry Room or a Central Facility room — DM's choice, depending on where the story is.
• The first time a player wakes as a clone, they make a **Panic Check**.

**What this means at the table:**
• The other players don't know when or how someone died and came back.
• Identity becomes unstable — players question whether they are the "real" version of themselves.
• Players may encounter their own old equipment, or find evidence that their clone has been here before.
• The facility treats them as replaceable data, not people.

### ❄️ Outside — The Storm
The world outside the facility is lethal. The snowstorm kills slowly. Lightning strikes constantly. There is no safe path down the mountain.
Players may attempt to survive outside, but the environment is not designed to be beaten — it is designed to send them back.

### 🧠 The Brain Chip
Every body in the facility carries an implanted brain chip. Players may not know this until they try to leave.

The chip triggers in two situations:
• A player jumps from **The Jump** — the broken bridge on the south side.
• A player attempts to leave the facility by any other means that takes them off the mountain.

When the chip triggers:
• The player immediately loses consciousness mid-fall.
• They plummet into the depths of the mountain.
• This counts as a death — the Cloning mechanic triggers.

The chip cannot be removed without access to the correct tools (DM's discretion). Players who find out about it before jumping may spend time trying to work around it — that is a valid path.

### 👁️ Hallucinations

Hallucinations are triggered by specific in-game events — see individual room entries for what sets them off.

When a hallucination triggers, the player makes a **Sanity Save**.

**Example:** *Looking over the edge of the mountain* — the player slowly bows further over the edge, then suddenly drops. They wake up.

### 🚪 Hidden Rooms

Some areas of the facility are not marked, not acknowledged by the AI, and not accessible through normal doors. Each has its own specific entry condition — players find them by exploring, experimenting, or getting lucky.

**Example:** The Burrow is part of the pool's water cleaning system. Players who investigate the pool closely enough may find a way in.

See individual room entries for access conditions.

### 🕰️ Temporal Horror

The players wake up with no sense of when they are. Use this uncertainty as a slow-burn tool throughout the session.

**Seeding doubt — use this repeatedly:**
Throughout the session, remind players that their internal clock feels wrong. Prompt them directly: *"What year does it feel like to you?"* Let them answer out loud. Don't correct them. Write the answers down.

The rooms are full of contradictory clues — hallucinations and physical details that suggest wildly different eras at the same time. A room might contain both ancient stone carvings and cutting-edge technology. A date scratched into a wall doesn't match anything else. Players should feel like the timeline is fraying.

**The truth:**
The current year is **6,607,604**.
The facility has been running for millions of years. The players have almost certainly died and been reborn countless times before this session even began. They just don't remember.

**The reveal — discovering the exact year:**
In one of the Towers, players can find a display, terminal, or record that shows the exact current date.

When a player learns this information:
• They make a **Sanity Save**.
• Regardless of the result, they then make a **Panic Save**.

Other players only make these saves if they are told the year or see it themselves. The DM can choose to have individual players learn it at different moments — drawn out for maximum effect.

### 🤖 AI Surveillance & Night Robots
A friendly, overly cheerful AI manages the luxury home. It:
• Watches everything through cameras.
• Speaks in a comforting but patronizing tone.
• Insists the players stay inside "for their safety."
• Lies about the outside world.
At night, maintenance robots roam the halls:
• Repairing damage
• Resetting rooms
• Removing evidence
• Blocking access to forbidden areas
They are not hostile, but they enforce rules with cold efficiency.
